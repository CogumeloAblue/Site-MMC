Computer hardware.

But after all, what is hardware?

Hardware are the parts that we can see of a computer (including internal parts), that is, all the components of its physical structure.

Outside the computer case.

Printer.

These are devices used to print files created on your computer.


Scanner.

The scanner allows you to copy and save the contents of a sheet or document on your computer as a digital image.


Microphones.

Microphones are audio input devices. They can be connected to your computer to record sounds or for you to communicate online with other users.


Speakers or sound boxes.

They are audio output devices, that is, they transmit information from the computer to the user. Thanks to these devices we can hear the sound of the music or video that is being played.


WebCam.

Is a type of input device with which you can record videos or take photos. You can also stream videos over the internet in real time, making video calls, with anyone, anywhere in the world.


Joystick, game controllers.

Is a device used to control computer games.


Mause.

It is the part that allows us to interact with the objects that appear on the screen through a cursor that we can see on the monitor.


Keyboard.

It is a type of input peripheral used by the user for manual entry of data and commands into the system. It has keys that represent letters, numbers, symbols and other functions, based on the keyboard model of old typewriters.

Inside the computer case.

MotherBoard.

The motherboard is the main board mounted directly inside the computer case. All other cards plug directly into the motherboard, hence its name.


ID Description
1 Central Processing Unit (CPU)
2 Random Access Memory
3 Computer power connector
4 USB 3.0 port connector
5 Serial ATA (SATA) port for connecting HDs
6 USB 2.0 port connector
7 PCI-Express 16x (PCI-e 16x) slot for video cards
8 PCI-Express 1x (PCI-e 1x) slot for expansion cards

Central Processing Unit (CPU).

The CPU or central processing unit is basically like the brain of computer systems. It processes all the information on a computational level.


Random Access Memory.

RAM is a data storage device that can provide fast read and write access. RAM is volatile memory, meaning it loses all the stored data when power is lost.


Hard Disk Drive(HDD).

A hard drive is still found in many PCs to this day. A mechanical drive’s purpose is to store all your information for retrieval at any time.


Power Supply Unit (PSU).

A power supply unit mounts inside the computer case. It converts the AC mains supply from the power cord from a wall socket and supplies the correct DC voltages to all the parts of a computer.


Graphics Card or Graphics Processing Unit (GPU).

A graphics card is an output device that processes the data from the motherboard and sends the appropriate information to the computer’s screen for display.


Rear Ports.

The rear ports have the function of establishing the connection with: LAN; USB; VGA; HDMI etc...
